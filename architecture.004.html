004 : Residential Development at Yau Tong

The studio calls for a residential development at Yau Tong, Hong Kong. The proposed development contains four high-rise residential blocks which clubhouse and mommunal open spaces. Drawing inspiration from the pulsing waves of the Victoria habour next to the project site, the design scheme presents a curvilinear design approach, with the use of undulating curves to create a rhythmic wave flowing across the elevation..

Studio: HPA || Role: Architectural Designer || Year: 2019-2020

"Architecture is about trying to make the world a little more like our dreams."

Bjarke Ingels

There are communal open spaces on 1/F with recreational facilities such as sitting out area and children play area.

The residential clubhouse is located at the central of site with roof gardens to enhance the overall greenery of the development.

Design Development Concept:

1. Ventilation channels have been reserved in the building plan. With reference to the potential odor source report of neighboring projects, the potential odor at the en- trance of the neighboring fish market according to the report data is less than 2 odor units. Therefore, it has fully complied with the assessment criteria of air quality impact and life hazard.

2. The concept of the design is to maximize the sea view. The width of each group of building is not more than 60m, and each group of buildings needs to reserve 15m spacing to meet the requirement of regulations. The residential landscape is oriented towards the open sea view in order for the residents to enjoy as much sea view as possible.

3. By integrating and coordinating with the existing houses next to the site, residences of site A can enjoy a permanent sea view. On the premise of complying with environment protection requirements and not opening ventilation windows to coastal pollution sources, the largest number of apartments towards the sea view will be provided to ensure that all the master rooms and living rooms will have sea views.

4. In order to reduce the impact of noise, the clubhouse and inner streets are used as barriers to reduce the im- pact of noise on the lower floors. Proper design skills are applied to reudece the use if sound insulation walls or componets to control construction costs.
